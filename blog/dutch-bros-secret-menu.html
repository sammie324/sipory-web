Dutch Bros Secret Menu and Straw Codes Decoded

March 202610 min read30+ Drinks · Straw Code · Broista Tips

Dutch Bros has one of the largest secret menus in coffee — 80+ unofficial drinks that have been created by customers and broistas over the years. Unlike Starbucks, where baristas rarely know secret menu names, Dutch Bros broistas generally know the popular ones by name. Say "Galaxy Fish" or "Bob Marley" at the window, and most broistas will know exactly what you mean.

This guide covers the 15 best secret menu drinks worth ordering, the straw color code myth, how to order like a regular, and which viral items to skip.

The Straw Color Code: Myth or Real?

Let's get this out of the way first, because it's the #1 searched Dutch Bros question. The legend says broistas use colored straws to send messages:

Pink
"You're pretty"
Blue
"You're cute"
Green
"You're ugly"
Yellow
"You're average"
Orange
"Wanna be friends"

The official answer: it's not real. Dutch Bros has publicly stated that straw colors are random — broistas grab whatever's closest. The "code" was invented by customers on TikTok and became a viral meme. Some broistas play along for fun (deliberately choosing pink for drive-thru compliments), but there's no official policy or training around straw colors.

That said, the myth is part of what makes Dutch Bros culturally sticky — it's a conversation starter, a TikTok content engine, and a reason people pay attention to details at the window. It's marketing that customers created for free.

How to Order Secret Menu Drinks

Dutch Bros' secret menu works differently from Starbucks' because of the broista culture:

Try the name first. Say "Can I get a Galaxy Fish?" Most broistas at most locations will know it. This works for the top 15–20 most popular items. If they don't recognize it, give the recipe.

Know the base. Every secret menu drink starts with a standard category: a Breve (espresso + half-and-half), a Freeze (blended), a Rebel (energy drink), a Frost (milkshake-style, no coffee), a Lemonade, or a Soda. Know which one your drink uses.

Ask the broista. This is the biggest difference from Starbucks — Dutch Bros broistas are trained to recommend drinks. If you say "I want something fruity and sweet but not coffee," they'll build something on the spot. The secret menu is really just a collection of their best custom creations.

The "surprise me" hack: Tell the broista your flavor preferences (fruity, chocolate, caramel, etc.) and say "surprise me." Dutch Bros culture embraces this — broistas enjoy creating custom drinks and often make their best secret menu items this way. It's the easiest way to discover something new without memorizing recipes.

The 15 Best Secret Menu Drinks

The Legends (Everyone Knows These)

Galaxy Fish Worth It
Base: Blue Rebel Energy Drink
Flavors: Strawberry + lime + passion fruit
The 2025 Bracket Challenge champion — voted #1 by Dutch Bros customers in a tournament-style competition. Fruity, tart, tropical, and visually striking with a galaxy-purple color. The passion fruit gives it depth that separates it from a standard strawberry Rebel. No coffee, ~152mg caffeine from the Blue Rebel base. This is the drink to start with if you've never tried the secret menu.
Say: "Can I get a Galaxy Fish Rebel?" (most broistas know it)
~$4.00–$5.50 · ~152mg caffeine
Bob Marley Worth It
Base: Breve (espresso + half-and-half) or Freeze
Flavors: Coconut + banana + dark chocolate
One of the oldest and most iconic Dutch Bros secret menu drinks. The coconut-banana-chocolate combination is tropical-meets-dessert — like a chocolate-covered banana smoothie with espresso. Works best as a Freeze (blended) in summer or a hot breve in winter. Rich and indulgent. Order with oat milk instead of half-and-half to lighten it up.
Say: "Can I get a Bob Marley Freeze?" (or Breve for hot)
~$5.50–$6.50 · ~150mg caffeine (espresso version)
White Zombie Worth It
Base: Breve or Freeze
Flavors: White chocolate + vanilla mocha
Sweet, creamy, and dessert-forward. The white chocolate + vanilla mocha combination creates a drink that tastes like white chocolate ganache in liquid form. This is the go-to for people who love Starbucks White Mocha but want a Dutch Bros version. Dangerously drinkable — the sweetness sneaks up on you.
Say: "Can I get a White Zombie Breve?"
~$5.50–$6.50 · ~150mg caffeine

The Fruity Ones (Non-Coffee)

Unicorn Blood Worth It
Base: Rebel or Lemonade
Flavors: Strawberry + white chocolate + almond
Pink, creamy, and sweet with a nutty undertone from the almond. As a Rebel, it's an energy drink with candy-like flavor. As a Lemonade, it's a refreshing summer drink. The white chocolate makes it creamier than a standard fruity Rebel. Great for people who like the Pink Drink at Starbucks but want more intensity.
Say: "Can I get a Unicorn Blood Rebel?" (or Lemonade)
~$4.00–$5.50 · ~152mg caffeine (Rebel)
Shark Attack It's Fine
Base: Blue Rebel
Flavors: Blue raspberry + lime + coconut
Bright blue, tropical, and sour-sweet. The blue raspberry makes this look striking in photos. Tastes like a blue slushie from a county fair — fun and nostalgic but very sweet. Better as a small than a large. The coconut smooths out the tartness.
Say: "Can I get a Shark Attack Rebel?"
~$4.00–$5.50 · ~152mg caffeine
Palm Beach Worth It
Base: Lemonade or Rebel
Flavors: Peach + mango + passion fruit
The most tropical drink on the secret menu. Peach-mango-passion fruit creates a flavor that tastes like a Hawaiian vacation. No chocolate, no cream — pure fruit layering. Exceptionally refreshing as a lemonade in hot weather. The best non-coffee, non-energy secret menu option.
Say: "Can I get a Palm Beach Lemonade?" (or Rebel for caffeine)
~$3.50–$5.00

The Chocolate/Dessert Category

Ninja Turtle Mocha It's Fine
Base: Mocha Breve
Flavors: White chocolate + crème de menthe (mint)
Mint chocolate chip ice cream in latte form. The crème de menthe adds a cool, minty freshness to the chocolate base. Divisive — you either love mint-chocolate or you don't. If you like Andes mints or Girl Scout Thin Mints, this is your drink.
Say: "Can I get a Ninja Turtle Mocha?"
~$5.50–$6.50 · ~150mg caffeine
Cookies and Cream Breve Worth It
Base: Breve
Flavors: White chocolate + dark chocolate + vanilla
The triple combination creates a flavor that genuinely mimics cookies and cream ice cream. The white chocolate provides sweetness, dark chocolate adds depth, and vanilla ties them together. This is the best dessert-category secret menu drink — it tastes more layered than most official menu items.
Say: "Can I get a Cookies and Cream Breve?"
~$5.50–$6.50 · ~150mg caffeine
Cookie Dough Freeze Worth It
Base: Freeze (blended)
Flavors: Chocolate macadamia nut (Annihilator base) + cookie dough syrup + whipped cream
Basically a drinkable cookie dough milkshake with espresso. The Annihilator base (chocolate macadamia nut) provides the nutty, chocolatey foundation, and the cookie dough syrup pushes it into full dessert territory. Order this as a Freeze — the blended texture is essential.
Say: "Can I get a Cookie Dough Freeze with Annihilator and cookie dough?"
~$6.00–$7.00 · ~150mg caffeine

The Classics (Old-School Favorites)

James Dean It's Fine
Base: Rebel or Lemonade
Flavors: Strawberry + peach + coconut
Named after the actor — cool, classic, fruity. Strawberry and peach blend into a sweet tropical profile, with coconut adding creaminess. It's similar to the Palm Beach but sweeter and less tart. A solid all-rounder but not as distinctive as the Galaxy Fish or Unicorn Blood.
Say: "Can I get a James Dean Rebel?"
~$4.00–$5.50 · ~152mg caffeine (Rebel)
Grasshopper It's Fine
Base: Freeze (blended)
Flavors: Crème de menthe + dark chocolate + whipped cream
The frozen version of mint chocolate. Tastes like a blended Thin Mint cookie. Better than the Ninja Turtle Mocha for mint-chocolate lovers because the frozen texture makes it feel more like a milkshake. Summer-only vibes.
Say: "Can I get a Grasshopper Freeze?"
~$5.50–$6.50 · ~150mg caffeine

Secret Menu vs. Official Menu: How They Differ

Here's the thing — Dutch Bros' official menu is already highly customizable. The Signature Faves (Golden Eagle, Annihilator, Kicker, Cocomo, 9-1-1) are already creative flavor combinations. The secret menu just takes it further by mixing flavors that aren't combined in the official lineup.

If you love the official menu, here's how secret menu drinks relate: the Bob Marley is like a Cocomo (coconut mocha) with banana added. The White Zombie is like a Kicker (Irish cream) but with white chocolate replacing the Irish cream. The Cookie Dough Freeze is like an Annihilator (chocolate macadamia nut) with cookie dough layered in. Thinking of secret menu drinks as "official menu + one twist" makes them less intimidating to order.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Dutch Bros have a secret menu?

Yes — 80+ unofficial drinks. Unlike Starbucks, broistas generally know popular items by name (Galaxy Fish, Bob Marley, White Zombie). Not every location knows every item, so having the recipe as backup helps.

What do Dutch Bros straw colors mean?

The straw color "code" (Pink = pretty, Blue = cute, etc.) is an unofficial myth created by customers on TikTok. Dutch Bros has confirmed straw colors are random. Some broistas play along for fun, but there's no official policy.

What is the most popular Dutch Bros secret menu drink?

The Galaxy Fish — the 2025 Bracket Challenge champion. It's a Blue Rebel with strawberry, lime, and passion fruit. Other top picks: Bob Marley, White Zombie, and Unicorn Blood.

Can you order secret menu drinks through the Dutch Bros app?

Not directly — the app doesn't have a secret menu section. Order at the window. Most broistas know popular items by name. The app is useful for paying and earning Rewards points on your order.
