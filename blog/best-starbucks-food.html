Best Starbucks Food Ranked: What to Order Beyond the Drinks

March 20269 min readRanked by Category · Calories · Drink Pairings

Food now accounts for roughly 25% of Starbucks US sales — yet most coffee blogs never cover it. The food menu has expanded significantly under Brian Niccol's "Back to Starbucks" plan, with new bakery items, a freshness initiative, and breakfast sandwiches that rival fast-casual chains. But the menu is also full of calorie traps: that Blueberry Muffin has more calories than a Big Mac.

This guide ranks the best food at Starbucks by category, with calories, protein counts, prices, and — most importantly — which drink pairs best with each item.

Top Picks by Category

Breakfast Sandwiches

1Bacon, Gouda & Egg Sandwich
370 cal · 19g protein · ~$4.95
The most popular Starbucks food item — and it earns it. Applewood-smoked bacon, aged gouda, and a Parmesan egg frittata on an artisan roll. The gouda melts into a smoky, sharp layer that elevates this above standard fast-food breakfast sandwiches. At 370 calories with 19g protein, the macros are reasonable for breakfast. Consistently warm and well-made across locations.
Pair with: Iced Brown Sugar Oatmilk Shaken Espresso — the brown sugar complements the smoky bacon
2Egg, Pesto & Mozzarella Sandwich New 2026
~380 cal · ~20g protein · ~$5.25
The best new food addition from the February 2026 menu update. Cage-free egg, basil pesto, mozzarella, and spinach on a toasted ciabatta. The pesto adds herby brightness that most Starbucks sandwiches lack — it tastes more "artisan café" than "chain coffee shop." Slightly more expensive than the Bacon Gouda but more complex flavor-wise.
Pair with: Iced Blonde Vanilla Latte — the vanilla won't compete with the pesto's herbaceous flavor
3Impossible Breakfast Sandwich
420 cal · 22g protein · ~$5.95
The best plant-based option. Impossible sausage patty, cage-free egg, and cheddar on a ciabatta. The Impossible patty is surprisingly close to real sausage — seasoned well with a meaty texture. At 22g protein, it's the highest-protein breakfast sandwich. The downside: it contains egg and cheese, so it's vegetarian but not vegan.
Pair with: Cold Brew with oat milk — keeps the plant-forward theme going

Egg Bites (Best for Health-Conscious)

1Egg White & Roasted Red Pepper Egg Bites
170 cal · 13g protein · ~$4.95 for 2 bites
The healthiest hot food at Starbucks. Sous vide egg whites with roasted red peppers and spinach in a cottage cheese base. Fluffy, flavorful, and only 170 calories for the pair. The protein-to-calorie ratio (13g per 170 cal) is excellent. Keto-friendly. The only downside: they're small — you might want two orders if you're genuinely hungry, which doubles the price.
Pair with: Nitro Cold Brew — 5 cal drink + 170 cal egg bites = a full 175-calorie breakfast with 13g protein
2Bacon & Gruyère Egg Bites
300 cal · 19g protein · ~$4.95 for 2 bites
The indulgent egg bite. More flavorful than the egg white version — the Gruyère and bacon add richness. At 300 calories and 19g protein, still reasonable. These taste closer to a quiche than a health food. Consistently the most popular egg bite flavor.
Pair with: Vanilla Sweet Cream Cold Brew — the sweet cream and bacon-Gruyère are a breakfast pairing dream
Egg bite hack: Order egg bites with an everything bagel (~300 cal, ~$2.25). Split the bites and put them inside the bagel like a sandwich. Total cost: ~$7.20. Total calories: ~470–600. It's essentially a protein-packed breakfast sandwich you assembled yourself, with better egg quality than the pre-made sandwiches.

Pastries and Bakery

1Butter Croissant
250 cal · 5g protein · ~$3.45
The reliable classic. Buttery, flaky, and consistently well-made. At 250 calories, it's one of the lowest-calorie bakery items — a meaningful difference versus the muffins (360–460 cal). Pairs with literally any coffee. Ask for it warmed for maximum flakiness. Starbucks reformulated their bakery under Niccol's freshness initiative in 2026, so the quality has improved.
Pair with: Caffè Latte — the most classic café combination on Earth
2Yuzu Citrus Cream Croissant Muffin New 2026
~350 cal · ~5g protein · ~$3.95
A croissant-muffin hybrid (in the "cruffin" tradition) with yuzu citrus cream filling. Flaky croissant exterior, soft muffin-like interior, bright citrus cream. This is Starbucks' most creative bakery item in years — the yuzu adds a tart, almost grapefruit-like brightness that's genuinely distinctive. Limited availability; grab it when you see it in the case.
Pair with: Iced Matcha Latte — citrus and matcha complement each other beautifully

Protein Boxes (Best for Lunch)

1Cheese & Fruit Protein Box
~470 cal · 16g protein · ~$6.25
The most balanced protein box: white cheddar, apple slices, grapes, multigrain crackers, and a mini peanut butter cup. It's not a meal replacement but a satisfying snack with varied textures and flavors. The peanut butter cup adds just enough sweetness. Best value among the protein boxes.
Pair with: Iced Chai Latte — the spices complement the fruit and cheese

Cake Pops and Treats

Frog Cake Pop Spring 2026
~170 cal · ~2g protein · ~$3.45
A vanilla cake pop shaped and decorated like a frog. Seasonal and collectible — it's the kind of item people buy for the Instagram photo first and the taste second. The vanilla cake is standard cake pop quality (fine, not spectacular). Kids love it. Available while spring supplies last.

The Calorie Traps to Avoid

ItemCaloriesWhat You'd ExpectWhy It's a Trap
Blueberry Muffin360~200Dense, sugary, more cake than muffin. A Big Mac is 550 cal.
Banana Nut Loaf420~250Essentially banana bread cake. 42g sugar per slice.
Chocolate Chip Cookie360~200One cookie = nearly a meal's worth of calories
Double Chocolate Brownie410~300Almost as caloric as the Bacon Gouda sandwich, with zero protein
Old Fashioned Glazed Doughnut480~300The highest-calorie single pastry item. 27g of fat.

The pattern: muffins, loaf cakes, and doughnuts are calorie bombs disguised as breakfast. A Blueberry Muffin (360 cal, 6g protein) has more than double the calories of Egg White Egg Bites (170 cal, 13g protein) with less than half the protein. If you're health-conscious, stick to egg bites and sandwiches; skip the bakery case unless you're treating yourself intentionally.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best food at Starbucks?

Bacon, Gouda & Egg Sandwich (370 cal, 19g protein, ~$4.95) is the most popular. The new Egg, Pesto & Mozzarella Sandwich is the most flavorful. Egg White & Red Pepper Egg Bites are the healthiest (170 cal, 13g protein).

What is the healthiest food at Starbucks?

Egg White & Roasted Red Pepper Egg Bites (170 cal, 13g protein) and Kale & Mushroom Egg Bites (230 cal, 15g protein). Avoid muffins (360–460 cal) and loaf cakes (400+ cal).

Does Starbucks have lunch food?

Food is available all day but breakfast-focused. Lunch options: Turkey Pesto Panini (~480 cal, 28g protein), Crispy Grilled Cheese (~520 cal), and protein boxes (various). No salads or traditional lunch entrées.

What new food does Starbucks have in 2026?

Egg, Pesto & Mozzarella Sandwich (February), Yuzu Citrus Cream Croissant Muffin, and Frog Cake Pop (spring seasonal). The bakery lineup was reformulated for freshness under CEO Brian Niccol.
