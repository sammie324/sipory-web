Dunkin' New Drinks 2026: Spring Menu, Dunkin' Zero, and Banana Drinks Ranked

March 20269 min read7 New Items · Energy Wars · Rankings

Dunkin' went all-in on banana for spring 2026. The March 4 menu drop includes four banana-flavored drinks, a new energy drink line (Dunkin' Zero), Berry Açaí Refreshers, and Wedding Cake Munchkins. It's the most aggressive Dunkin' seasonal launch in years — and it beat Starbucks' Energy Refreshers to market by five weeks.

Here's every new item ranked, with prices, caffeine counts, and honest verdicts on what's worth ordering.

Dunkin' Zero is Dunkin's direct entry into the energy drink market — zero sugar, approximately 20 calories per medium, and 145mg of caffeine. It launched March 4, five weeks before Starbucks' Energy Refreshers arrive on April 7. Available in 6 flavors at launch, it's positioned against Celsius, GHOST, and Alani Nu rather than traditional energy drinks like Red Bull and Monster.

The value proposition is strong: at approximately $3.49 for a medium (24 oz), Dunkin' Zero is cheaper than a 12 oz can of Celsius (~$2.50–$3.00) while giving you twice the liquid. It's also significantly cheaper than the upcoming Starbucks Energy Refreshers (~$5.75–$6.25). For budget-conscious energy drinkers, Dunkin' Zero is the best deal in the chain energy war.

How it compares: Dunkin' Zero (145mg/24oz, 20 cal, ~$3.49) vs Starbucks Energy Refresher (est. ~145mg/16oz, ~110–150 cal, ~$5.75) vs Dutch Bros Rebel (~152mg/16oz, ~80–250 cal, ~$3.50–$4.00). Dunkin' Zero wins on price and calories. Starbucks wins on fruit-juice flavor. Dutch Bros wins on customization and flavor variety. See our Energy Refreshers guide for the full comparison.

The Banana Drinks, Ranked

1. Banana Puddin' Cloud Latte Worth It
Medium · ~$5.29 · ~118mg caffeine · Spring 2026 seasonal
The star of the spring menu. Banana pudding-flavored syrup with espresso and milk, topped with sweet cloud cold foam that mimics the whipped cream layer on actual banana pudding. The banana flavor is distinctly "banana pudding" rather than plain banana — there's a vanilla-wafer warmth to it. The cloud foam texture makes it feel more premium than a standard flavored latte. If you have any affinity for banana pudding as a dessert, this is a strong recommend.
Say: "Can I get a medium Banana Puddin' Cloud Latte?"
2. Banana Shakin' Espresso Worth It
Medium · ~$4.99 · ~210mg caffeine · Spring 2026 seasonal
Dunkin's Shakin' Espresso format (their answer to Starbucks' Shaken Espresso) with banana syrup. Espresso shots shaken with banana flavoring and oat milk. Higher caffeine than the Cloud Latte, less sweet, more coffee-forward. The banana acts as a natural sweetener that complements espresso surprisingly well — similar to how banana bread and coffee pair together. Better for people who want flavor + function rather than a dessert drink.
Say: "Can I get a medium Banana Shakin' Espresso?"
3. Bananarama Matcha It's Fine
Medium · ~$4.69 · ~80mg caffeine · Spring 2026 seasonal
Banana + matcha is a polarizing combination. The banana sweetness softens the earthy matcha, creating something that tastes like a green smoothie with a banana twist. If you already like matcha, this is an interesting variation. If you're matcha-neutral or matcha-skeptical, the banana doesn't add enough to convert you. The color is a slightly yellowish green — not as Instagram-friendly as the purple ube drinks at Starbucks.
Say: "Can I get a medium Bananarama Matcha?"
4. Monkey Business Cloud Latte It's Fine
Medium · ~$5.29 · ~118mg caffeine · Spring 2026 seasonal
Chocolate + banana with cloud foam — essentially a chocolate-banana latte. The concept is "chocolate-covered banana" in drink form. It works, but the chocolate and banana flavors compete rather than complement, making it taste muddled compared to the cleaner Banana Puddin'. If you want chocolate + banana, the Bob Marley at Dutch Bros (coconut + banana + dark chocolate) is better executed. This is the weakest of the four banana drinks.
Say: "Can I get a medium Monkey Business Cloud Latte?"

The Rest of the Spring Menu

Berry Açaí Refreshers Worth It
Medium · ~$4.09 · ~66mg caffeine · Spring 2026 seasonal
Dunkin's Refresher lineup gets a berry-açaí addition. Bright berry flavor, light caffeine from green tea extract, and a refreshing tartness that makes it a solid afternoon drink. Competes directly with Starbucks' Strawberry Açaí Refresher — similar flavor profile, lower price (~$4.09 vs ~$4.95). For the full Starbucks Refresher comparison, see our Refreshers guide.
Say: "Can I get a medium Berry Açaí Refresher?"
Wedding Cake Munchkins Worth It
~$2.49–$4.99 (varies by count) · Spring 2026 limited time
Not a drink, but worth mentioning — wedding cake-flavored Munchkins with a vanilla glaze. Sweet, buttery, and addictive. Pairs well with any of the banana drinks or a simple iced coffee. Limited time, so grab them while they last.

What's Still on the Menu (Best Non-Seasonal Picks)

The spring menu is exciting, but Dunkin's best drinks are on the permanent menu. If you're visiting Dunkin' for the first time or want reliable options alongside the seasonal items:

Iced Caramel Swirl Latte — #1 editor pick and Dunkin's best-selling specialty drink. The Caramel Swirl (thick, creamy) versus a Shot (thin, sugar-free) makes a huge difference at Dunkin'. See our full Dunkin' ordering guide for the complete breakdown of Swirls vs Shots.

Cold Brew with Sweet Cold Foam — Dunkin's answer to Starbucks' Vanilla Sweet Cream Cold Brew. Smooth, sweet, and consistently good. The cold foam is lighter than Starbucks' version.

$6 Meal Deal — Medium coffee + Bacon Egg & Cheese + hash browns. The best food-plus-coffee value at any chain. Available through the Dunkin' app. Pairs well with any spring drink — swap the medium coffee for a Banana Shakin' Espresso at minimal upcharge.

The Rewards Play

Every spring drink earns Dunkin' Rewards points (10 pts/$1). At 500 points (~$50 spent), you get a free drink. The $6 Meal Deal also earns points. Dunkin' Rewards earns free drinks faster than Starbucks ($50 vs $100) — and there's often a "try the new menu" bonus points promotion in the app during the first week of a seasonal launch. Check the app for current offers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What new drinks does Dunkin' have for spring 2026?

Banana Puddin' Cloud Latte, Monkey Business Cloud Latte, Banana Shakin' Espresso, Bananarama Matcha, Dunkin' Zero energy drink (6 flavors), Berry Açaí Refreshers, and Wedding Cake Munchkins. All launched March 4, 2026.

What is Dunkin' Zero?

Dunkin's new energy drink — zero sugar, ~20 calories per medium, 145mg caffeine, 6 flavors. At ~$3.49, it's cheaper than Starbucks Energy Refreshers and most canned energy drinks. It targets the Celsius/GHOST audience.

Is the Banana Puddin' Cloud Latte good?

Yes — it's the standout spring drink. Banana pudding flavor with cloud cold foam that mimics the dessert. Overwhelmingly positive reviews. Skip if you dislike artificial banana flavor.

How does Dunkin' Zero compare to Celsius?

Dunkin' Zero: 145mg caffeine, 24oz, 20 cal, ~$3.49. Celsius: 200mg, 12oz, 10 cal, ~$2.50–$3.00. Dunkin' Zero has more liquid, less caffeine per ounce, and similar pricing. Both are ultra-low calorie. Dunkin' Zero is fresh-made; Celsius is canned.
